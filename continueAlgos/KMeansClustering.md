K-Means Clustering (Unsupervised Learning)

This is the entry point into Unsupervised Learning, where the data does not have pre-defined labels or "correct answers."

The Concept: The algorithm groups data into K distinct clusters. It identifies "centroids" (the center of a cluster) and assigns points to the nearest one, iterating until the groups are stable.

The Project: Market Segmentation

Goal: Group anonymous customers into personas for a marketing team.

Implementation: Take a dataset of customer spending scores and annual income. Use K-Means to identify clusters like "High Spend/Low Income" or "Frugal/High Income."
